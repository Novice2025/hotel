Essential Vocabulary & Phrases for Your Stay

Sample Conversation

Receptionist:—Good afternoon, San Felice Hotel. May I help you?

Mrs Ryefield:—Yes. I'd like to book a room, please.

Receptionist:—Certainly. When for, madam?

Mrs Ryefield:—March the 23rd.

Receptionist:—How long will you be staying?

Mrs Ryefield:—Three nights.

Receptionist:—What kind of room would you like, madam?

Mrs Ryefield:—Er... double with bath. I'd appreciate it if you could give me a room with a view over the lake.

Receptionist:—Certainly, madam. I'll just check what we have available. . . Yes, we have a room on the 4th floor with a really splendid view.

Mrs Ryefield:—Fine. How much is the charge per night?

Receptionist:—Would you like breakfast?

Mrs Ryefield:—No, thanks.

Receptionist:—It's eighty four euro per night excluding VAT.

Mrs Ryefield:—That's fine.

Receptionist:—Who's the booking for, please, madam?

Mrs Ryefield:—Mr and Mrs Ryefield, that's R-Y-E-F-I-E-L-D.

Receptionist:—Okay, let me make sure I got that: Mr and Mrs Ryefield. Double with bath for March the 23rd, 24th and 25th. Is that correct?

Mrs Ryefield:—Yes it is. Thank you.

Receptionist:—Let me give you your confirmation number. It's: 7576385. I'll repeat that: 7576385. Thank you for choosing San Felice Hotel and have a nice day. Goodbye.

Mrs Ryefield:—Goodbye.

Reporting Common Issues

Dirty linen/sheets: "The sheets on my bed are dirty." or "The linen in my room hasn't been changed."

No water from the tap: "There's no water coming from the tap in my bathroom." or "The water supply seems to be out of order in my room."

Strange smell: "I've noticed a strange smell in my room." or "There's a strong, unusual odor coming from the ventilation."

Noise from next door: "There is a lot of noise coming from the room next door." or "I'm being disturbed by a loud noise from the adjacent room."

TV not switching on: "The television in my room is out of order." or "The TV is not working; it won't switch on."

Lost key card: "I seem to have lost my key card." or "I've misplaced my room key and need a new one."

Useful Phrases for Requests

For an iron: "Would I please get an iron and an ironing board sent to my room?"

For a remote control: "We're missing the remote control for the TV. Would you send one up to the room?" or "Is it possible to get a remote control for the air conditioner?"

For a remote control battery: "The remote control battery seems to be dead. Would someone please bring a new one to my room?"

For a large towel: "Would it be possible to get an extra large towel for my room?"

For a water leak: "There appears to be a water leak from the roof in my room. Would someone come and take a look at it?"

For a city map: "Would you have a city map available at the front desk?"
